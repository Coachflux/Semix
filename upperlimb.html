2. Cell Theory ‚Äì The Foundation of Biology üìú

All living things are made of cells! This big idea came from many scientists.

Key discoveries:

  • 1665 ‚Äì Robert Hooke saw "cells" in cork under microscope
  • 1670s ‚Äì Leeuwenhoek saw living cells (bacteria, protozoa)
  • 1838 ‚Äì Schleiden: All plants are made of cells
  • 1839 ‚Äì Schwann: All animals are made of cells
  • 1855 ‚Äì Virchow: "All cells come from pre-existing cells"

Three main rules today:

  1. All organisms are made of one or more cells
  2. Cell is the basic unit of structure, function, and life
  3. All cells come from other cells (via division)

3. Cell Structure ‚Äì Plant vs Animal Cells üî¨

Cells are tiny living units. Plant üåø and animal üê± cells are similar but different!

Plant Cell Parts

  • Cell wall ‚Äì Rigid protection & shape (cellulose)
  • Chloroplasts ‚Äì Photosynthesis (make food from light)
  • Large central vacuole ‚Äì Storage & keeps plant firm
  • Others: nucleus, mitochondria, ribosomes, ER, Golgi, cytoplasm

Functions of Key Organelles in Plant Cells: The cell wall provides structural support and prevents bursting. Chloroplasts convert sunlight into energy via photosynthesis. The large vacuole stores nutrients and maintains turgor pressure for rigidity. The nucleus controls activities and holds DNA. Mitochondria produce ATP energy. Ribosomes build proteins. ER and Golgi handle transport and packaging.

Animal Cell Parts

  • No cell wall, no chloroplasts, no large vacuole
  • Has centrioles for cell division
  • Others same as plant: nucleus, mitochondria, ribosomes, ER, Golgi, cytoplasm

Functions of Key Organelles in Animal Cells: The cell membrane regulates substance entry/exit. Centrioles organize spindles during division. The nucleus directs cell functions with DNA. Mitochondria generate energy through respiration. Ribosomes synthesize proteins. ER modifies and transports proteins/lipids. Golgi packages and secretes materials. Cytoplasm hosts reactions.

Plant vs Animal ‚Äì Quick Comparison

  • Both: membrane, nucleus, cytoplasm, mitochondria, ribosomes, ER, Golgi
  • Plant only: cell wall, chloroplasts, large vacuole
  • Animal only: centrioles
